THE OPERATING MODEL STRATEGY:

LINKING WHERE YOU ARE NOW TO WHERE YOU WANT TO BE IN THE FUTURE

Evidence of Finance’s ability to transform has still proven somewhat elusive with a disproportionate amount of teams still reliant on manual processes during the month end close and time spent processing transactions rather than harnessing the data to improve performance.

Today’s digital technology paradigm is where data processing and storage is becoming exponentially cheaper and a vast array of low code and intuitive analytical tools. However, the existing digital landscape for legacy finance teams still assumes that processor and memory costs are prohibitively expensive (monthly batch processing of subledger feeds) and all digital initiatives are restricted to IT (institutionalised workarounds on spreadsheets).

Does your Finance team have a competitive edge or is it a case of just dreaming of the following:

  • Focused understanding on measuring business performance rather than the majority of resource focused on manual data integration
  • Self-service data and analysis delivered in real time
  • Planning analysis which is data driven more than ‘rule of thumb’ guesswork
  • Foster meaningful business relationships between Finance and the rest of the organisation
  • Able to deliver rapid business insights to enable the business to pivot with confidence
  • Proactively drive enhanced operational and related Financial performance
  • Have a culture of Data and Financial literacy across the whole business so that effective communications are enabled through common language
  • A culture of managing risks rather than avoiding them where failure means fast feedback, losses are minimised and lessons learned applied going forward

With all the available digital tools and technology it should be easier than ever for Finance to transform. Why then is this not the case? Perhaps because Finance Leaders have too many options that it’s difficult to identify the ‘best’ one.

In this case where it limits progress we should consider simply aiming for ‘better’ rather than ‘best’. Other reasons that may prevent securing the support for a transformation programme include:

  • Weak Business Case ROI, where smaller teams have limited costs base for savings and cannot link Finance transformation to business growth
  • The costs and risks for Finance to move away from legacy architecture increase as more time passes
  • Low risk tolerance and no room for experimentation because risk of failure linked with change is higher
  • Complex business systems with many different touch points that need to be fully analysed end to end
  • Finance might be perfectly happy with their existing systems and see no urgency to change
  • Fear of committing to a solution due to vendor lock-in
  • No experience with a proven change methodology and limited change expertise

The change process has been defined by various proven methodologies and frameworks i.e. Waterfall (PMP / PRINCE2), AGILE (SCRUM, KANBAN), Lean Six Sigma, Change Management etc. with the same basic components, but greater emphasis on different parts. As with Finance these are a collection of tools, each with their own unique strengths and limitations, best placed to handle certain specific problems.

However, the fundamental components to Transformation success are:


Charter
Define the need to change and lay out a clear Vision for the desired future state

Stakeholders
Identify and communicate to those impacted by the proposed change to get alignment and buy-in

Business Case
Analyse the benefits vs the costs and determine key resources needed to ensure a tangible Return on Investment

Kick off
Get formal approval for funding and initiate the project

Planning
Get all the necessary inputs and develop realistic plans that can be monitored

Resources
Create and coach effective teams to lead the change initiative, ensure that sufficient funding is available to achieve the plan

Governance
Monitoring project progress in terms of timing, cost and scope plus ongoing Risk and Issue management

Analysis
Analyse the gap between the current state and design the path to the desired future state

Measure
Establish effective KPI’s that focus on leading indicators with an empahsis on progressing with quality

Reporting
Ensure progress status reports are accurate, timely and easily accessible to stakeholders

Communication
Continuously keep everyone informed so issues can be effectively addressed

Project Closure
Monitor post-launch and follow up to ensure stakeholder satisfaction and that benefits are realised

There are many essential traits to change leadership, but key to leading successful transformations are communication, focus, ownership, integrity, curiosity, creative problem-solving, persistence and resilience.
