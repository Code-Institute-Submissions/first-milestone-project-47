THE OPERATING MODEL STRATEGY:

LINKING WHERE YOU ARE NOW TO WHERE YOU WANT TO BE IN THE FUTURE

Evidence of Finance’s ability to change has still proven somewhat limited with a disproportionate amount of reliance on manual processes during the month end close and time spent processing transactions rather than harnessing the data to improve performance.

Today’s digital technology paradigm is where data processing and storage is becoming increasingly cheaper and a vast array of low code and intuitive analytical tools. Does your Finance team have a competitive edge or is it a case of just dreaming of the following?:

  • Focused understanding on measuring business performance rather than the majority of resource focused on manual transaction processing
  • Foster meaningful business relationships between Finance and the rest of the organisation
  • Planning analysis which is data driven more than ‘rule of thumb’ guesswork
  • Able to deliver rapid, insightful business insights to enable the business to pivot
  • Processes that enable positive outcomes by enabling organisation responsiveness
  • Proactively drive enhanced operational and related Financial performance
  • Have a culture of Data and Financial literacy, not just within the Finance team, but across the business so that effective communications are enabled through common language
  • A culture of risks where failure is fast feedback and losses are minimised as lessons learned are integrated and applied going forward

With all the available digital tools and technology it should be easier than ever for Finance to transform. Why then is this not the case? Perhaps because Finance Leaders have too many options that it’s difficult to identify the ‘best’ one.

In this case where it limits progress we should consider simply aiming for ‘better’ rather than ‘best’. Other reasons that may prevent securing the support for a transformation programme include:

  • Weak Business Case ROI, where smaller teams have limited costs base for savings and cannot link Finance transformation to business growth
  • The costs and risks for Finance to move away from legacy architecture increase as more time passes
  • Low risk tolerance and no room for experimentation because risk of failure linked with change is higher
  • Complex business systems with many different touch points that need to be fully analysed end to end
  • Finding the ideal solution that fits the company best possible fear of committing to a solution that becomes later becomes the least desirable (vendor lock-in)
  • Finance might be perfectly happy with their existing systems and see no urgency to change
  • No experience with a proven change methodology and limited expert resources available

The change process has been defined by various proven methodologies and frameworks i.e. Waterfall (PMP / PRINCE2), AGILE (SCRUM, KANBAN), Lean Six Sigma, Change Management etc. with the same basic elements, but greater emphasis on different parts. Like Finance these are a collection of tools, each with their own unique strengths and limitations, best placed to handle certain specific problems.

However, the fundamental components to an effective Transformation plan are:


Charter
Identify the need to change and lay out the Vision of the desired future state

Stakeholders
Identify and communicate to those impacted by the proposed change for engagement and support

Business Case
Analyse the benefits and estimate the costs determine what key resources will be needed to provide a tangible Return on Investment

Kick off
Get formal approval for funding and initiate the project

Project Plan
Get all the necessary inputs and develop realistic plans that can be monitored

Resources
Create and coach effective teams to lead the change initiative, ensure that sufficient funding is available to achieve the plan

Governance
Monitoring project progress plus ongoing Risk and Issue management

Analysis
Evaluate the gap between the current state and design the path to the desired future state

Measure
Establish effective KPI’s that focus on leading indicators

Reporting
Make progress status accurate, timely and easily accessible to stakeholders through data mastery

Communication
Continuously keep everyone informed so issues can be effectively addressed

Project Closure
Monitor post-launch and follow up to ensure stakeholder satisfaction and that benefits are realised

There are many essential traits to change leadership, but key to leading successful transformations are communication, focus, ownership, integrity, curiosity, problem solving, persistence and resilience.
